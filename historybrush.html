History Brush

Technique: All the meerkats and the background are black and white, except for one meerkat, which is the main subject of the image. This creates a strong visual impact by immediately drawing the focus to the meerkat that is in color.

Purpose: This technique is used to direct the viewer's attention to the most important or most interesting part of the photo. It can also add a dramatic, artistic or abstract feel.
  • Step 1: Creating a Snapshot: A Snapshot of the original, colored image is taken in the History panel. This is the "colored" starting point that the brush will return to.
  • Step 2: Converting the Image to Black and White: The entire image (for example, with an Adjustment Layer) is completely converted to black and white (Desaturate).
  • Step 3: Applying the History Brush: The History Brush tool is selected and the brush size is adjusted. The brush is carefully applied to the area we want to be colored (the meerkat in front here).
  • Result: The areas where the History Brush was applied will return from black-and-white to the colored snapshot taken in step 1., while the black-and-white layer untouched by the brush remains as is.
